<p><p>The Ventricular Septal Defect (VSD) Occluder Device market is categorized into two main types based on the location of the defect: Membranous VSD Occluders and Muscular VSD Occluders. Membranous VSD Occluders are designed to close defects in the membranous septum, typically found in the upper region of the ventricles. Muscular VSD Occluders, on the other hand, target defects in the muscular part of the septum, often requiring specialized devices for effective closure. Both types are vital for improving patient outcomes in congenital heart disease.</p></p>
