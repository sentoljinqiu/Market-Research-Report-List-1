<p><p>The wastewater pumps market features two primary types: submersible and dry-type pumps. Submersible pumps are designed to operate underwater, efficiently moving wastewater from deep sources, making them ideal for applications like sewage systems and wastewater treatment plants. In contrast, dry-type pumps are installed above the fluid level, using a suction mechanism to draw up waste. They are easier to maintain and operate in applications where flooding is less of a concern, such as in drainage and industrial settings.</p></p>
