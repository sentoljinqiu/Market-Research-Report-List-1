The Sodium Metal Process utilizes sodium metal and methanol as raw materials, allowing for a direct synthesis approach that yields high purity. In contrast, the Caustic-Based Process employs caustic soda and methanol, often seen as a more cost-effective method. Both processes serve various applications in biodiesel production, pharmaceuticals, and chemical manufacturing, catering to differing industrial needs.</p></p>
